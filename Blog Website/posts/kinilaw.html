Kinilaw

Well-served Kinilaw

Kinilaw is a Filipino appetizer made with raw, cubed fish in a dressing based on vinegar mixed with garlic, ginger, and sometimes coconut milk, creating a refreshing and zesty dish that tantalizes the taste buds. This traditional dish is often enjoyed as a starter or side, showcasing the vibrant flavors of the Philippines' coastal cuisine. While kinilaw is typically made with fresh fish like tuna or mackerel, it can also include a variety of seafood, such as shrimp or squid. The key to a great kinilaw lies in the freshness of the ingredients, as the fish is "cooked" in the acidity of the vinegar, resulting in a delicate texture and bright flavor. Accompanied by chopped onions, tomatoes, and chili peppers, kinilaw is a perfect balance of tangy, spicy, and savory notes. It is often served chilled, making it an ideal dish for warm weather or festive gatherings. With its vibrant colors and bold flavors, kinilaw not only delights the palate but also embodies the rich culinary heritage of the Philippines, celebrating the bounty of the sea in every bite.

Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb oxtail (or beef/tripe)
  • 6 cups water
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 1/2 cup peanut butter
  • 1/4 cup ground peanuts (optional)
  • 1 eggplant, sliced
  • 1 cup banana hearts, sliced
  • 1 cup string beans, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Bagoong (fermented shrimp paste) for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, boil the oxtail (or chosen meat) in water until tender, about 1.5 to 2 hours.
  2. Remove the meat and set aside, reserving the broth.
  3. In the same pot, saut√© the onion until translucent.
  4. Add the reserved broth back to the pot and stir in the peanut butter and ground peanuts, mixing well.
  5. Add the cooked meat and bring to a simmer.
  6. Add the eggplant, banana hearts, and string beans, cooking until the vegetables are tender.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve hot with bagoong on the side.
